§3.2 The Chain Rule —— L1×1 + L2×2

L2：chain rule × quotient rule 從未示範。現有 Ex 3.4／3.5 內層導數都一步可得（\(2x\) 或 \(\cos x\)）；沒有任何例子的「內層導數本身是一道 Ch2 規則的計算」。

3.2-E1 highL2　chain × quotient

Differentiate \( \sqrt{\dfrac{x-1}{x+2}} \).

The outermost operation is the square root, so by Strategy 3.1 take \(f(u)=\sqrt{u}=u^{1/2}\) with inner \(u=\dfrac{x-1}{x+2}\). The chain rule gives

\[ \frac{d}{dx}\sqrt{\frac{x-1}{x+2}}=\frac{1}{2\sqrt{(x-1)/(x+2)}}\cdot\frac{d}{dx}\!\left(\frac{x-1}{x+2}\right). \]

The inner derivative is now a quotient-rule computation (§2.5):

\[ \frac{d}{dx}\!\left(\frac{x-1}{x+2}\right)=\frac{(x+2)\cdot 1-(x-1)\cdot 1}{(x+2)^2}=\frac{3}{(x+2)^2}. \]

Substituting, and writing \(\dfrac{1}{2\sqrt{(x-1)/(x+2)}}=\dfrac{\sqrt{x+2}}{2\sqrt{x-1}}\),

\[ \frac{d}{dx}\sqrt{\frac{x-1}{x+2}}=\frac{\sqrt{x+2}}{2\sqrt{x-1}}\cdot\frac{3}{(x+2)^2}=\frac{3}{2\sqrt{x-1}\,(x+2)^{3/2}}\qquad(x>1). \]

The point is that the chain rule does not finish the job by itself: the factor it asks for — the derivative of the inside — is a small problem of its own, here a quotient rule.

L2：chain rule × product rule 從未示範，且 \(\cos^2x=[\cos x]^2\)「三角冪是 composition」的辨識沒被單獨擺到 product 結構裡。

3.2-E2 mediumL2　chain × product

Differentiate \( y=(1+x^{2})\cos^{2}x \).

The outermost operation is a product, so start with the product rule (§2.5):

\[ y'=(2x)\cos^{2}x+(1+x^{2})\,\frac{d}{dx}\bigl(\cos^{2}x\bigr). \]

The second factor's derivative needs the chain rule, since \(\cos^{2}x=[\cos x]^{2}\) is a composition (outer \((\cdot)^{2}\), inner \(\cos x\)):

\[ \frac{d}{dx}\bigl(\cos^{2}x\bigr)=2\cos x\cdot(-\sin x)=-2\sin x\cos x. \]

Therefore

\[ y'=2x\cos^{2}x-2(1+x^{2})\sin x\cos x. \]

Reading the structure: the product rule splits the work into two terms, and the chain rule supplies the slope factor inside the second term. The two rules cooperate — neither alone differentiates this function.

why：補「product 切兩項、chain 供其一項內層斜率」的接合，與 E1 的 chain×quotient 成對。標 medium：與 E1 同屬「chain＋Ch2 規則」家族，若只收一個，E1（內層更不平凡）優先。
L1（正式缺口）：Remark 3.2 的 Leibniz 形 \(\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{dy}{du}\cdot\frac{du}{dx}\) 全節零示範。現有例都走函數記號 \(f'(g(x))\,g'(x)\) 的機械代入，從未走「rate × rate」推理。

3.2-E3 mediumL1　Leibniz 形（rate × rate）

In a harbour, the amount of kelp \(K\) depends on the urchin population \(U\) (urchins eat kelp), and \(U\) in turn depends on the otter population \(O\) (otters eat urchins). Using the chain rule, decide whether \(\dfrac{dK}{dO}\) is positive or negative.

First read off the sign of each link. More urchins means less kelp, so \(K\) decreases as \(U\) increases: \(\dfrac{dK}{dU}<0\). More otters means fewer urchins, so \(\dfrac{dU}{dO}<0\). The chain rule in Leibniz form (Remark 3.2) chains the two rates:

\[ \frac{dK}{dO}=\frac{dK}{dU}\cdot\frac{dU}{dO}. \]

A product of two negative numbers is positive, so \(\dfrac{dK}{dO}>0\): more otters ultimately means more kelp. This matches the plain-language reasoning — otters cut the urchins, and fewer urchins lets the kelp recover — and the chain rule is exactly the formal version of that two-step argument: each \(dy/du\) factor carries the sign of one link, and the signs multiply along the chain.

why：補 Remark 3.2 的 Leibniz「rate 複合」示範——chain rule 的 meaning（非 mechanics）那一面。標 medium：屬概念型，與 §3.2 偏計算的既有例風格略異；若你偏好全計算型，可只收 E1／E2。我個人覺得這條值得收——Leibniz 形目前真的零示範，而這題優雅。

§3.3 Applications of the Chain Rule —— L2×1

L2：Strategy 3.2 明列 log diff 適用於「a sprawling product and quotient of many factors」，但 3 個 log-diff 例（3.7 \(x^x\)、3.8 \(2^x\)）全是「變數指數」型——「乘積化成對數和」的那半從未被任一例示範。

3.3-E1 highL2　log-diff 乘積式

Differentiate \( y=(x+1)(x^{2}+1)^{2}(x^{3}+1)^{3} \) for \(x>0\), using logarithmic differentiation.

The product rule would demand three (then more) nested applications; instead take logs. Since \(y>0\),

\[ \ln y=\ln\!\bigl[(x+1)(x^{2}+1)^{2}(x^{3}+1)^{3}\bigr]. \]

The laws of logarithms (Strategy 3.2, step 2) turn the product into a sum and pull each exponent out front:

\[ \ln y=\ln(x+1)+2\ln(x^{2}+1)+3\ln(x^{3}+1). \]

Differentiate both sides with respect to \(x\). On the left, \(\dfrac{d}{dx}\ln y=\dfrac{y'}{y}\) by the chain rule. On the right, each term is a logarithm of a simple expression, so \(\dfrac{d}{dx}\ln u=\dfrac{u'}{u}\) gives one fraction per factor:

\[ \frac{y'}{y}=\frac{1}{x+1}+\frac{2(2x)}{x^{2}+1}+\frac{3(3x^{2})}{x^{3}+1}=\frac{1}{x+1}+\frac{4x}{x^{2}+1}+\frac{9x^{2}}{x^{3}+1}. \]

Solve for \(y'\) and substitute \(y\) back:

\[ y'=(x+1)(x^{2}+1)^{2}(x^{3}+1)^{3}\left[\frac{1}{x+1}+\frac{4x}{x^{2}+1}+\frac{9x^{2}}{x^{3}+1}\right]. \]

Notice that each factor of the original product contributed exactly one term to the bracket — log-diff trades one hard product-rule computation for several easy logarithmic derivatives added together.
