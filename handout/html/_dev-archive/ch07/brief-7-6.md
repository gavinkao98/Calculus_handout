# Direction brief — §7.6 Arc Length

Canon variant (Stewart ET 9e §8.1). Lightweight, bullet-level; the ⑤ direction-conformance contract. Written 2026-07-18 per PLAN-ch07 (D3: C¹ arc length theorem proved — the seam-ledger premise "C¹ 弧長定理" closes HERE; run-1 B1 bridging sentence mandatory).

- **Canon inventory (§8.1):** what "length of a curve" should mean (a string laid along the curve, pulled straight); inscribed polygon: chord lengths \(\sum\lvert P_{i-1}P_i\rvert\), refine → definition of arc length as the limit; the smooth-case formula \(L=\int_a^b\sqrt{1+[f'(x)]^2}\,dx\) via MVT per chord; worked lengths (curves engineered so \(1+f'^2\) integrates); the arc length function \(s(x)=\int_a^x\sqrt{1+[f'(t)]^2}\,dt\) and its derivative; the differential \(ds\) with \(ds^2 = dx^2 + dy^2\) mnemonic.
- **Thinness:** canon is *enough* on the derivation shape; Stewart waves at two spots this book must nail: (i) WHY the MVT sample points \(x_i^*\) are legitimate — this is exactly Def 6.2's any-sample-point clause + Thm 6.1 (the book's own machinery, cite them); (ii) the hypothesis bridge (run-1 B1): MVT 4.12 needs continuity on each closed strip + differentiability on each open strip; the theorem assumes MORE — \(f'\) continuous on \([a,b]\) (one-sided at the endpoints) — because the INTEGRAND needs it; say explicitly that the stated hypothesis grants both needs. → the proof is short and fully honest with zero new fences.
- **Scope & depth:** 標準嚴謹 with the chapter's second full proof. Per ③-D3 final: mint **Definition 7.5 (Arc length)** for the GRAPH of \(f\) continuous on \([a,b]\), \(a<b\): the polygon length over the regular partition, \(L=\lim_{n\to\infty}\sum_{i=1}^n\lvert P_{i-1}P_i\rvert\) — a **finite** limit if it exists; length is unsigned (no reversed-orientation convention; a degenerate interval has \(L=0\), one clause). Then **Theorem 7.3 (Arc Length Formula)**: if \(a<b\) and \(f\in C^1([a,b])\) — the notation defined IN PLACE: \(f\) continuous on \([a,b]\), differentiable with \(f'\) extending continuously to the endpoints (one-sided there); call such \(f\) <em>smooth on \([a,b]\)</em> — then \(L\) exists and equals \(\int_a^b\sqrt{1+[f'(x)]^2}\,dx\). Proof: chord \(i\) has length \(\sqrt{\Delta x^2+(\Delta y_i)^2}\); MVT 4.12 on \([x_{i-1},x_i]\) gives \(\Delta y_i = f'(x_i^*)\Delta x\), so the chord = \(\sqrt{1+f'(x_i^*)^2}\,\Delta x\); the polygon length is a Riemann sum for \(g=\sqrt{1+f'^2}\) with particular sample points; \(g\) continuous (composition/sum of continuous; \(f'\) continuous by hypothesis) ⟹ Thm 6.1 + Def 6.2's any-choice clause: the sums converge to \(\int_a^b g\), REGARDLESS of which sample points a rule hands us — so the polygon lengths converge and the limit is the integral. Bridging sentence (B1) placed in the proof's opening: the blanket hypothesis " \(f'\) continuous on \([a,b]\)" hands MVT its per-strip needs AND makes the integrand continuous. Then: **arc length function** \(s(x)\), its derivative \(s'(x)=\sqrt{1+f'(x)^2}\) by FTC-1 (Thm 6.3; integrand continuous — hypothesis available), and the **differential** \(ds=\sqrt{1+(dy/dx)^2}\,dx\) tied back to §5.3 differentials; the \(ds^2=dx^2+dy^2\) picture (infinitesimal Pythagoras) as mnemonic WITH honest status (a mnemonic for the proved formula, not a derivation — one clause). **Introduce-before-use:** nothing B-class; distance formula A-class.
- **Load-bearing intuition (one):** length is measured by a straightedge — but a curve fits no straightedge. Collision: a curve has no formula for length at all until we make one; chords UNDER-measure every arc they cut, yet as the chords shorten they hug the curve. The definition accepts the limit of chord-polygons as what length MEANS (same modeling honesty as Def 7.2); the theorem then computes it. Secondary reading for the integrand: \(\sqrt{1+f'^2}\) is the local stretch factor — flat where \(f'=0\) (integrand 1: length ≈ run), steep where \(\lvert f'\rvert\) is large.
- **Worked examples (toolkit-constrained: perfect squares / u-sub only — no trig sub, no ∫sec):**
  - **Ex 7.18** — \(y = x^{3/2}\) from \(0\) to \(1\) (canon's classic): \(1+f'^2 = 1+\tfrac94 x\), u-sub: \(L = \bigl[\tfrac{8}{27}(1+\tfrac94 x)^{3/2}\bigr]_0^1 = \tfrac{8}{27}\bigl(\bigl(\tfrac{13}{4}\bigr)^{3/2}-1\bigr) = \tfrac{13\sqrt{13}-8}{27}\).
  - **Ex 7.19** — \(y = \dfrac{x^3}{6}+\dfrac{1}{2x}\) on \([1,2]\) (perfect-square family): \(f' = \tfrac{x^2}{2}-\tfrac{1}{2x^2}\), \(1+f'^2 = \bigl(\tfrac{x^2}{2}+\tfrac{1}{2x^2}\bigr)^2\), so \(L=\int_1^2\bigl(\tfrac{x^2}{2}+\tfrac{1}{2x^2}\bigr)dx = \tfrac{7}{6}+\tfrac{1}{4} = \tfrac{17}{12}\). Positivity of the root's argument checked (the bracket is positive on [1,2] — hypothesis hygiene line).
  - **Ex 7.20** — arc length FUNCTION: for \(y=\dfrac{x^2}{8}-\ln x\) on \([1,x]\) (uses §6.4's \(\int dx/x\)): \(f' = \tfrac{x}{4}-\tfrac1x\), \(1+f'^2=\bigl(\tfrac{x}{4}+\tfrac1x\bigr)^2\), \(s(x) = \int_1^x\bigl(\tfrac t4+\tfrac1t\bigr)dt = \tfrac{x^2-1}{8}+\ln x\); check \(s(1)=0\), \(s'(x)=\tfrac x4+\tfrac1x=\sqrt{1+f'(x)^2}\) (FTC-1 in action); read off \(ds\).
