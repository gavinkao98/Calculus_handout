# Direction brief — §7.1 Areas Between Curves

Canon variant (Stewart ET 9e §6.1). Lightweight, bullet-level; the ⑤ direction-conformance contract. Written 2026-07-18 per PLAN-ch07. This fragment also carries the **Chapter 7 opener** (first `<article>`).

- **Canon inventory (§6.1):** region between \(y=f(x)\) and \(y=g(x)\) with \(f\ge g\) on \([a,b]\): strip sum \(\sum[f(x_i^*)-g(x_i^*)]\Delta x\) → \(A=\int_a^b(f-g)\,dx\); worked cases: two explicit curves over a given interval; region enclosed by two parabolas (intersection-finding); curves that cross (total area via \(\int\lvert f-g\rvert\), split at crossings); regions sliced horizontally (\(x\) as a function of \(y\), right−left).
- **Thinness:** canon is *enough* on the formula + cases. *Thin*: why negativity of \(f, g\) is harmless (sign-shift invariance — raise both graphs, the gap is unmoved); the consistency check against Def 6.1; the net-vs-total echo of §6.2/§6.4 when curves cross. → make the gap-not-height intuition the lead; wire crossing-curves to the established \(\int\lvert v\rvert\) move.
- **Scope & depth:** 標準嚴謹, computation-forward. Mint **Definition 7.1** (area between curves, \(f\ge g\), both continuous); existence of the limit via Thm 6.1 (the sum is a Riemann sum of the continuous \(f-g\)) — one sentence, cite explicitly. Consistency vs Def 6.1 when \(g=0\) — one sentence. **Introduce-before-use:** nothing B-class new (antiderivatives from the §6.4 table: \(e^x\), powers, \(\sin\), \(\cos\); symmetry Thm 6.8 for the horizontal-slice example). Chapter opener names the slice–approximate–sum–refine schema (PLAN D7); §7.1's own opener instantiates it with the two-curve strip.
- **Load-bearing intuition (one):** the strip's height is the **gap** \(f-g\), not the height above the axis. Collision: below the axis, "area under the curve" readings go signed/wrong, yet the region between two curves is plainly there — raising both graphs by the same constant changes neither the region's area nor \(f-g\), so subtracting graphs *first* is what makes negativity harmless.
- **Worked examples (concrete → diagnostic → construct → symmetry):**
  - **Ex 7.1** — area between \(y=e^x\) and \(y=x\) over \([0,1]\) (given interval, verify which curve is on top; answer \(e-\tfrac32\)).
  - **Ex 7.2** — region *enclosed* by \(y=x^2\) and \(y=2x-x^2\) (intersections are Step 1; answer \(\tfrac13\)).
  - **Ex 7.3** — total area between \(y=\sin x\) and \(y=\cos x\) on \([0,\pi/2]\) (curves cross at \(\pi/4\); split; answer \(2\sqrt2-2\)).
  - **Ex 7.4** — region enclosed by \(x=y^2\) and \(x=2-y^2\) (horizontal slices; even integrand → Thm 6.8 halves the work; answer \(\tfrac83\)).
- **History / application:** *application* = the velocity-gap reading (if \(v_A\ge v_B\), the area between the velocity curves is the lead built up — Net Change §6.4 echo), one short paragraph, `expansion:application`, non-decorative. No history block (§6.1's Archimedes note already carries the chapter's ancient-roots line; do not repeat at same depth).
- **figure_opportunities** (mark now, draw at M2): (a) region between two curves with one representative strip of height \(f-g\) (essential; graph); (b) crossing curves with the two sub-regions shaded and the crossing marked (strong; graph); (c) horizontal-slice region \(x=y^2\) vs \(x=2-y^2\) with a horizontal strip (strong; graph). Domain facts: (a) generic curves, no crossing inside \([a,b]\); (b) crossing at \(\pi/4\) for sin/cos; (c) intersections at \(y=\pm1\), region symmetric about the \(x\)-axis.
- **Emphasis / takeaway:** concept pivot = *subtract the graphs before integrating; the integrand is a gap*; portable skill = set up an area integral: find intersections, choose the slicing direction, keep top−bottom (right−left) honest, split where boundaries swap.
