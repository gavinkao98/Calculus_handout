擬補全文（authored，本書語域；sympy 已驗 V=8π/15）：
Example. The region enclosed by \(y=x\) and \(y=x^{2}\) is rotated about the horizontal line \(y=2\). Find the volume.
Solution. As in Example 7.7 the region runs over \([0,1]\) with \(y=x\) on top and \(y=x^{2}\) below, and lies entirely beneath \(y=2\). Rotating about that line, each radius is a vertical distance to \(y=2\). The boundary farther from the axis is the lower curve, so it gives the outer radius \(R=2-x^{2}\); the nearer boundary gives the inner \(r=2-x\). Then \[ V=\pi\int_{0}^{1}\!\bigl[(2-x^{2})^{2}-(2-x)^{2}\bigr]dx=\pi\int_{0}^{1}\!\bigl(x^{4}-5x^{2}+4x\bigr)dx=\pi\Bigl[\tfrac{x^{5}}{5}-\tfrac{5x^{3}}{3}+2x^{2}\Bigr]_{0}^{1}=\tfrac{8\pi}{15}. \] The same region about the \(x\)-axis gave \(\tfrac{2\pi}{15}\) (Example 7.7); a different axis makes a different solid. Only where the radii were measured from changed — exactly the discipline the Caution insisted on.
落地連動：把現 Ex 7.7 收尾那句軸平移散文（我 M3 P-7.2-a 剛改過的那句）收成一句 lead-in（「the same region about \(y=2\) makes a different solid — Example 7.8」），避免與新例重複；然後 cascade（上方 warn）。圖：prose＋現 Fig 7.7 已足懂；小 schematic（畫出 \(y=2\) 與兩條垂直半徑）可另作 M2 follow-up，非本 gate。

Take \(f=v\), the velocity of a moving object; then \(f_{\text{ave}}=\tfrac{1}{b-a}\int_a^b v\,dt=\tfrac{s(b)-s(a)}{b-a}\) by the Net Change Theorem (§6.4) — total displacement over elapsed time, exactly the average velocity of elementary kinematics. The levelling-height rectangle is that everyday average, read for a continuously varying quantity.

7.6-a · §7.6（Ex 7.18 後） · history HIGH（已查證） 建議採用
缺口：§7.6 opener 說「no formula at all — not a poor one, none」，但無 history 標記兌現此修辭；且未解釋 Ex 7.18 為何偏挑 \(y=x^{3/2}\)。提案（Ex 7.18 solution 後一句）：
The curve of Example 7.18 is no arbitrary choice. The graph of \(y=x^{3/2}\) is the semicubical parabola \(y^{2}=x^{3}\), and in 1657 the nineteen-year-old William Neile made it the first algebraic curve ever to have its length computed — a result published by his teacher Wallis. Descartes had held, in his Géométrie, that the ratio of a curved line to a straight one "cannot be discovered by human minds"; Neile's rectification found one such length exactly, just as this section's opening promised.
