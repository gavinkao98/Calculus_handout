u18 · shm_compute — A Weight on a Spring

Source · storyboard say:（LOCKED；數學渲染）
Let us put the new derivatives to work on something moving. {show step.0} A weight on a spring has height $s(t)=\sin t$. {show step.1} Velocity is its derivative, $s'(t)=\cos t$. {show step.2} Acceleration is the next derivative, $s''(t)=-\sin t$. {show result} But $-\sin t$ is just $-s(t)$ -- at every instant the weight is pushed back toward rest. That relation, $s''=-s$, is the signature of simple harmonic motion; sine and cosine are exactly the functions that describe it.
